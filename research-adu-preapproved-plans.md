# SD County Free Pre-Approved ADU Plans

Research compiled 2026-03-04 at 1:00 AM.

## Overview

The **County of San Diego** (not the City — important distinction) offers **6 free pre-approved building plans** for Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs) and Single Family Dwellings (SFDs). These plans are approximately **85% complete** — users fill in project-specific information.

**URL:** https://www.sandiegocounty.gov/content/sdc/pds/bldg/adu_plans.html
**Contact:** 858-495-5382 or PDS.ADUquestions@sdcounty.ca.gov

## ⚠️ Jurisdiction Note

These plans are for **unincorporated areas of the County only**. The City of San Diego is a separate jurisdiction. However:
- The plans themselves are useful reference for what a complete ADU plan set looks like
- Under AB 1332, cities are encouraged/required to create similar pre-approval programs
- San Diego residents often don't know the difference between City and County

## Size → Fee Bracket Implications

From our user research, size matters for fees:
- **Under 500 sq ft** — Exempt from school fees in many cases
- **Under 750 sq ft** — Reduced impact fees
- **Over 800 sq ft** — Must comply with FAR (floor area ratio)
- **Up to 1,200 sq ft** — Maximum for detached ADU

So the plan options map to these brackets:
- **Plan F** (600 sq ft) — reduced fees bracket
- **Plan E** (800 sq ft) — on the edge of FAR requirement
- **Plan D** (1,000 sq ft) — full fees, under max
- **Plans A/B/C** (1,200 sq ft) — maximum detached ADU size

## AB 1332 Context

California AB 1332 (Gov Code §65852.27) requires local agencies to develop pre-approved ADU plan programs. SANDAG tracks which cities in SD County have programs:
- **County of San Diego** — 6 plans (documented above)
- **City of San Marcos** — has a Permit Ready ADU (PRADU) program
- **City of San Diego** — status unclear, may not have a formal pre-approved program yet

This is a product opportunity: if the City doesn't have pre-approved plans, we can flag county plans as a reference and note the jurisdiction difference.
