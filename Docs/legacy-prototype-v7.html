Building an ADU requires a building permit in San Diego
An ADU is new construction — it always requires a building permit, structural review, and inspections. There's no way around this one.
Why it matters: Building without a permit means no inspections for safety. If you ever sell, the buyer's inspector will flag it. You'd either tear it out or pay for retroactive permits — which cost more and aren't guaranteed to be approved.

The good news: ADUs in San Diego are approved "by right" (ministerially) — meaning no public hearing, no neighbor approval needed. It's paperwork, not politics.

What kind of ADU works for you?

Each option has different costs, timelines, and requirements. Pick one to explore.

🏗️
New detached ADU
A standalone structure in your backyard. Most flexibility on size and layout. Requires new foundation, utilities, the works.
Up to 1,200 sq ft $150k–$350k to build 6–12 months
🔗
Attached ADU
An addition connected to your existing house. Shares a wall, potentially shares some utilities. Slightly cheaper than detached.
Up to 1,200 sq ft $120k–$280k to build 5–10 months
🚗
Garage conversion
Convert your existing garage into a living space. Cheapest and fastest option — no new foundation needed. You lose your garage.
Size of your garage $80k–$180k to build 3–6 months
🏠
Junior ADU (JADU)
A small unit (≤500 sq ft) carved out of your existing house or attached garage. Kitchenette only, no full kitchen. You can build a JADU + a regular ADU on the same lot.
≤500 sq ft $40k–$100k to build 2–4 months
